What is Maria Shriver's Biography?

Maria Shriver is an American journalist, author, and former First Lady of California. Born on November 6, 1955, in Chicago, Illinois, she grew up in a politically active family. Shriver graduated from Georgetown University with a degree in American Studies and began her career in broadcasting as a reporter and anchor. Over the years, she has worked for major networks including NBC, where she became the anchor for the "Sunday Night News." Her career highlights have included covering significant national and international events, which have undoubtedly contributed to her public persona and financial success.
