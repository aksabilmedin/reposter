What is Jessica Lange's Early Life Like?

Born on April 20, 1949, in Cloquet, Minnesota, Jessica Phyllis Lange was the first of three children to Alfred and Dorothy Lange. Growing up in a close-knit family, she was raised in a modest home, where her love for the arts began to bloom. Lange's interest in performance was nurtured by her mother, an aspiring poet, who encouraged her to explore her creative side.

After graduating from high school, Lange pursued her passion for acting at the University of Minnesota, where she majored in drama. However, her aspirations took her beyond the confines of academia as she ventured to Paris to work as a model. This experience not only exposed her to the world of fashion but also opened doors to her acting career.

How Did Young Jessica Lange Transition to Acting?

Jessica Lange's transition from modeling to acting was both organic and serendipitous. After returning from Paris, she landed her first significant role in the 1976 film "King Kong," where she played the leading lady, Dwan. Her portrayal captured the attention of audiences and critics alike, marking the beginning of her illustrious career.

Following "King Kong," Lange continued to showcase her talent in various films, earning her first Academy Award nomination for her role in "Frances" (1982). This biographical drama showcased her remarkable ability to portray complex characters, further solidifying her status as a rising star in Hollywood.

What Are Some Iconic Roles of Young Jessica Lange?

  • Frances (1982): Lange's portrayal of actress Frances Farmer earned her an Academy Award nomination and showcased her depth as an actress.
  • Tootsie (1982): Starring alongside Dustin Hoffman, Lange's performance as a soap opera star solidified her comedic talents, earning her another Academy Award nomination.
  • Blue Sky (1994): This film brought Lange her first Academy Award win for Best Actress, as she portrayed a troubled woman dealing with the complexities of her life.
  • American Horror Story (2011-Present): Lange's role in this anthology series marked a new phase in her career, showcasing her versatility and earning her critical acclaim.
