What is a Cleft Palate and How Does it Affect Individuals?

A cleft palate is a congenital condition that occurs when there is an opening in the roof of the mouth due to incomplete fusion of the tissue during fetal development. This condition can significantly impact an individual's ability to eat, speak, and even hear, depending on the severity of the cleft. For many, it requires multiple surgeries and therapies to correct and manage the condition.

How Common is Cleft Palate?

Cleft palates are relatively common, affecting approximately 1 in every 1,000 live births. The prevalence can vary based on geographic location, ethnicity, and other factors. In some cultures, the occurrence of cleft palates is significantly higher, leading to varying societal perceptions and support systems for affected individuals.
