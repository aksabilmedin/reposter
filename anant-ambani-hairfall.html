What Are the Common Causes of Hairfall?

Hairfall can occur due to a variety of reasons, ranging from genetics to lifestyle choices. Understanding these factors is crucial in addressing the issue effectively. Here are some common causes:

  • Genetics: Hereditary hairloss can be a significant factor, often referred to as androgenetic alopecia.
  • Stress: High-stress levels can lead to a condition known as telogen effluvium, where hair follicles enter a resting phase.
  • Poor Nutrition: A diet lacking essential nutrients can result in hair thinning and loss.
  • Medical Conditions: Certain medical issues, like thyroid disorders, can also contribute to hairfall.

When it comes to hairfall, there are numerous myths that can mislead individuals. Here are some common misconceptions:

  • Myth 1: Washing hair frequently causes hairfall.
    Fact: Regular washing helps keep the scalp clean and does not directly contribute to hairloss.
  • Myth 2: Wearing hats or caps leads to hairfall.
    Fact: This is largely unfounded; hairfall is primarily related to genetics and health.

    Fact: Women also face hairloss, often due to hormonal changes and other factors.
